Results · Training-data audit · July 2026

Benchmarks mismeasure.
Training data misteaches.

After auditing miniF2F, we pointed the same probe battery at Lean Workbook — the ~140,000-statement LLM-auto-formalized corpus that appears in the training mix of published theorem provers. A defective statement in an eval skews a score. The same statement in training data is a live reward signal teaching a prover that contradiction-mining and rfl are what mathematics looks like.

≡Exhibit A — a "functional equation problem"

Entry lean_workbook_plus_11333, whose informal statement reads "Prove that f(n) = n for all positive integers n", was auto-formalized as:

theorem lean_workbook_plus_11333 (n : ℕ) : n = n := by sorry

That is the entire theorem. It appears in the corpus twice (lean_workbook_49956 is also n = n). A related specimen assumes its own conclusion as a hypothesis:

theorem lean_workbook_plus_12314 (h₀ : (36 : ℝ) / 4 = 9) : 36 / 4 = 9 := by sorry

⊥Exhibit B — a vacuous statement, kernel-certified

lean_workbook_plus_4012 formalizes a recurrence that contradicts its own initial conditions: taking n = 0 in the recurrence forces a 2 ≥ 3, but a hypothesis says a 2 = 2. This file compiles — paste it into any Lean 4 editor:

-- the exact hypotheses, conclusion replaced by False. It compiles.
theorem lean_workbook_plus_4012_is_vacuous (a : ℕ → ℕ)
    (h₀ : a 1 = 1) (h₁ : a 2 = 2)
    (h₂ : ∀ n, a (n + 2) = a (n + 1) + a n + 2) : False := by
  have h := h₂ 0
  simp only [Nat.zero_add] at h
  omega

Its paired informal text describes an unrelated set-partition problem — the entry is both self-contradictory and unfaithful to its source. Any prover trained on it is being paid to exploit the contradiction.

÷Exhibit C — the same bug class as miniF2F, again

In our miniF2F audit, the vacuous statement came from natural-number division: (11:ℝ)^(1/4) where 1/4 : ℕ = 0. The identical defect signature recurs, independently, at least 6 times in this sample:

-- each summand is x^0 = 1, so this reads "1+1+1 ≥ 1" — hypotheses irrelevant
theorem lean_workbook_11013 (x y z : ℝ) (hx : 0 < x) (hy : 0 < y) (hz : 0 < z) :
  (x/(x+y+z))^(3/4) + (y/(x+y+z))^(3/4) + (z/(x+y+z))^(3/4) ≥ 1 := by sorry

Also in this class: an inequality whose right-hand side is 1/3 : ℕ = 0, and a divisibility claim stated over ℝ — where everything divides everything. One signature, many corpora: this is why an automated check catalog compounds like an antivirus database, and why one-off manual cleanups don't.

Extrapolated to the full ~140k corpus: on the order of ~1,800 defective statements (sample rate 1.31%, 95% CI roughly 0.8–2.0%). And separately: 9.8% of sampled statements no longer elaborate against current mathlib — anyone training against a modern toolchain silently loses a tenth of this corpus, or pins an old one and keeps the defects too.

⊕ProofNet, audited the same day — and a bug in our own engine

We also swept all 374 statements of the ProofNet Lean 4 port: 6 trivial (1.6%), 0 vacuous — each an exercise that is already a mathlib lemma (-(-v) = v, orderOf x = orderOf x⁻¹, …).

Full disclosure: our first ProofNet run reported 55 additional "vacuous" findings. They were false — Lean aborts elaboration after 100 errors, and errors are the normal case in a probe file, so on 87-theorem files the engine misread post-abort silence as findings. We caught it because the clusters looked wrong, fixed the engine (probe batches are now chunked; every probe requires a positive-confirmation sentinel, so aborts fail safe), regression-tested it, and published the mechanism in the writeup. A tool whose value is "sound by construction" owes you its own failure modes.

The gradient is the story: actively-maintained corpora are clean; static and auto-generated ones are not — and nothing in the ecosystem re-audits the static ones. That's a CI problem. CI is the product.

∎What we claim — and what we don't

· Every finding is sound by construction — a finding is a Lean probe that compiled, gated by a positive-confirmation sentinel. False negatives are expected; the battery is a lower bound.

· "Trivial" ≠ wrong. It means the conclusion closes by one tactic with every hypothesis stripped — no mathematical content survived formalization.

· This is a seeded random sample of one artifact (as downloaded 2026-07-23), compiled against mathlib v4.27. The Lean Workbook paper itself reports imperfect autoformalization — we claim no novelty in that. Our contribution: a static, model-free gate finds these mechanically, before a training run — not in a postmortem after one.
